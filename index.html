1. Introduction

Mad Trader ("the App", "we", "our") is a personal trading compounding planner and journal developed for individual use. This Privacy Policy describes exactly what data is collected, by whom, and how it is used. By using Mad Trader, you agree to the terms of this Privacy Policy.

2. What data Mad Trader collects

Mad Trader itself does NOT collect, store, or transmit any of your personal or financial data to our servers. We have no servers.

However, the App integrates third-party SDKs (Firebase Crashlytics and PostHog Analytics now, and Google AdMob in a future update) that collect limited technical data as described in Section 4. This is standard for free apps and is disclosed in full below.

3. Your trading data — local storage

All data you enter into Mad Trader — including capital amounts, trading results, P&L entries, journal notes, strategies, and psychology tags — is stored exclusively on your device using a local SQLite database. This data:

  • Never leaves your device unless you explicitly trigger a cloud backup (see Section 5)
  • Is never accessible to us or any third party
  • Can be exported as a JSON file at any time from Settings
  • Can be permanently deleted via Settings → Reset All Data

Photo Library (optional): If you choose to attach a chart screenshot to a journal entry, the App accesses your device's photo library so you can select an image. Selected images are copied into the App's private local storage only — they are never uploaded anywhere unless you separately trigger a Google Drive cloud backup. The App never accesses your photo library unless you explicitly tap "Add Image" on a journal entry.

4. Third-party services & SDKs

The App uses the following third-party services. Each collects data independently under their own privacy policies, linked below.

Google AdMob (Free users only — not yet active)
Active in: planned for free tier, not yet enabled Will collect device data for ads once enabled

AdMob is not active in the current version of the App. This section describes how it will work once ads are enabled in a future update. Once active, free-tier users will see banner ads on the Dashboard and Settings screens. AdMob will collect:

  • Device advertising ID (Android Ad ID)
  • Device model, OS version, screen size
  • IP address (used to infer general location for ad targeting)
  • App usage signals (which screens were viewed, when)

This data will be used to serve relevant advertisements. It is collected by Google LLC and governed by Google's Privacy Policy. You will be able to opt out of personalised ads at any time via your device's Settings → Privacy → Ads → Opt out of Ads Personalisation.

Pro subscribers will see no ads once this is enabled.

Firebase Crashlytics (All users)
Active in: all tiers Collects crash and stability data

Firebase Crashlytics helps us identify and fix app crashes. It collects:

  • Crash logs and stack traces (code-level error details, not your trading data)
  • Device model, OS version, and app version at time of crash
  • A randomly generated installation UUID (not linked to your identity)
  • Timestamp of the crash

Crashlytics does NOT collect your trading data, journal entries, capital amounts, or any financial information. All crash reports are anonymous. Data is collected by Google LLC and governed by Firebase's Privacy Policy.

PostHog Analytics (All users)
Active in: all tiers Collects feature-usage events

We use PostHog to understand which features are actually used, so we know what to improve. On each session it sends a small number of lightweight events:

  • app_opened — when the app is launched
  • plan_created — planner mode and duration (no capital amounts)
  • journal_entry_added — market and trade direction only (no prices, P&L, or notes)
  • export_triggered — export format used (PDF/Excel)
  • backup_completed — whether a backup was local or cloud
  • upgrade_screen_viewed — that the Pro screen was opened

Each event is tied to a random ID generated on first install and stored only on your device — it is not your name, email, or Google account, and cannot be used to identify you personally. No trading data, journal text, capital amounts, or P&L figures are ever sent. Governed by PostHog's Privacy Policy.

Google Sign-In & Google Drive (Optional — user-initiated only)
Active in: opt-in only

See Section 5 for full details. Google Drive backup is only active if you choose to sign in with your Google account in Settings. Nothing is sent to Google Drive by default.

5. Optional cloud backup (Google Drive)

Mad Trader offers an optional cloud backup feature powered by Google Drive. This is entirely opt-in. It is not enabled by default and requires you to manually sign in with your Google account from Settings → Cloud Backup.

If you enable cloud backup:

  • Your trading plans, day rows, and journal entries are stored in a private AppData folder in your own Google Drive account
  • This AppData folder is hidden from your regular Drive view and only Mad Trader can read or write to it — not us, not any other app
  • You can restore your data or sign out (which ends the Drive connection) at any time from within the App
  • If you uninstall the App, the AppData backup is automatically deleted by Google along with the app's private storage
  • Governed by Google's Privacy Policy for the Drive/Sign-In portion of this feature

If you never sign in with Google, no data is ever sent to Google Drive.

6. Permissions

Mad Trader explicitly blocks camera, location, contacts, and microphone access. The following permissions are requested only when you use related features:

  • Storage / Files: To export and import backup JSON files to/from your device
  • Photos: Only if you choose to attach a chart screenshot to a journal entry. See Section 3.
  • Notifications: Only if you turn on Daily Reminder, Journal Nudge, or Weekly Summary in Settings → Notifications. Off by default. All scheduling happens locally on your device — no data is sent anywhere to deliver these reminders.
  • Internet: Required by Crashlytics (crash reports), PostHog (analytics events), optional Google Drive cloud backup, and AdMob once ads are enabled in a future update

No other permissions are used.

8. Children's privacy

Mad Trader is not directed toward children under the age of 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has used this app and data has been collected, contact us immediately.

Separately, because the App deals with real financial planning and trading concepts, it is intended for users aged 18 and above.

9. Your rights

You have full control over your data:

  • Access: All your data is visible within the App at all times
  • Export: Export everything as a JSON file via Settings → Backup
  • Delete: Permanently delete all local data via Settings → Reset All Data
  • Opt out of personalised ads: Android Settings → Privacy → Ads → Opt out of Ads Personalisation
  • Cloud backup removal: Sign out of Google from Settings → Cloud Backup to disconnect the App from your Drive. Your backup is also automatically deleted by Google if you uninstall the App, since it lives in the App's private AppData folder.
